A UX (User Experience) Librarian focuses on improving how users interact with the library's digital and physical services. Their primary role is to assess and enhance the usability, accessibility, and overall experience of the library's website, online catalogs, databases, and other interfaces. They conduct user research, such as surveys and usability testing, to gather feedback and identify areas for improvement. The UX Librarian collaborates with web developers, digital services teams, and librarians to implement design changes that make resources more intuitive and accessible. Key skills include user research methods, UX design principles, and a deep understanding of library systems and user needs.
